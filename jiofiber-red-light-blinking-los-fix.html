JioFiber Red Light Blinking (LOS): What It Means and How to Fix It

A blinking red LOS light on a JioFiber ONT means the fiber signal has been interrupted between the street cable and your ONT device. LOS stands for Loss of Signal. In most cases the cause is a fiber cable that's been disconnected or slightly pulled from the SC/APC connector on the ONT — it only needs to click back in. If that's not it, Jio needs to check the signal from their end.

💬 From experienceThe SC connector on the ONT is the small rectangular fiber plug — usually green or blue — that connects the fiber cable from outside into the device. It's the first thing to check. A properly seated connector clicks into place and doesn't wiggle. If it seems seated but LOS is still blinking, call Jio — the fault is likely somewhere on the line, not in your flat.

What Does the Red Blinking LOS Light Mean?

On JioFiber routers and ONT (Optical Network Terminal) devices, the red blinking light specifically corresponds to the LOS indicator — Loss of Signal.

What's really happening, LOS means your JioFiber equipment is not receiving any optical signal from Jio’s fibre optic network. The router itself is powered and functioning — which is why the red light is blinking rather than the device being completely dead — but it cannot detect the light signal that should be travelling through the fibre optic cable to your home.

Here’s how JioFiber actually works: unlike traditional broadband that sends electrical signals through copper wires, JioFiber transmits data using pulses of light through a thin glass fibre cable. This fibre runs from Jio’s local distribution point (called an OLT — Optical Line Terminal) all the way into your home, connecting to the small white or black box called an ONT (Optical Network Terminal) or directly to your JioFiber router depending on your setup.

The LOS error appears when the ONT or router stops detecting those light pulses. The four primary reasons this happens:

  • The fibre optic connector has become loose or disconnected. The small SC/APC connector — a green-tipped plug — that connects the fibre cable to your ONT or router can work loose from vibration, accidental pulling, or being nudged during cleaning. Even being 2mm from fully seated is enough to block the light signal entirely.
  • The fibre cable has been bent too sharply, kinked, or physically damaged. A sharp bend smaller than 30 mm radius will crack the glass core inside and block the light signal permanently. Even a small kink the size of a ₹10 coin is enough.
  • Jio is experiencing an outage or maintenance in your area. Signal disruptions at the OLT level will cause LOS on every customer connected to that node simultaneously.
  • Dust or contamination on the optical port is scattering the light signal before it can be detected.

The good news: causes 1, 2, and 4 are all fixable at home. Cause 3 resolves itself once Jio restores service.

Light travels from Jio’s OLT exchange through the fibre cable into your home. Any break anywhere in this chain — a loose SC/APC connector, a kinked cable section, or a fault at Jio’s splitter — stops all light pulses and triggers the red LOS blink on your ONT. Fixes 1–5 address each possible break point in order.
This is the blinking red LOS light on a JioFiber ONT. It appears at the bottom of the device as a single red LED. The device is powered and functioning — the blinking red specifically indicates Loss of Signal on the optical fibre input, not a hardware failure of the ONT itself.

What You’ll Need

You need very little for most of these fixes:

  • Your JioFiber router or ONT device
  • A clean, dry lint-free cloth (for cleaning the fibre connector — if needed)
  • A torch or flashlight to inspect the fibre cable routing
  • Your smartphone with Jio mobile data (to check for outages and contact support if needed)
  • About 10–20 minutes of your time

No screwdrivers, no technical equipment, and absolutely no tools required for the most common fixes.

5 Fixes — Fastest to Most Thorough

Follow the order — the quickest wins are at the top.

1Restart the JioFiber Router and ONT — 60 Seconds, Not 10

Before inspecting any cables, always start with a clean restart. A temporary signal glitch at Jio’s end — lasting a few seconds — can trigger an LOS state on your device that does not clear itself automatically. A restart forces the device to reattempt the optical connection from scratch.

  1. Locate the power adapter of your JioFiber router or ONT device.
  2. Unplug the power adapter from the wall socket completely.
  3. Wait a full 60 seconds. This allows the device’s optical receiver to fully power down and reset — a 10-second wait is not sufficient for this particular step.
  4. Plug the power adapter back into the wall and observe the indicator lights as the device boots up.
  5. Watch specifically for the LOS light — on most JioFiber ONT models this is a dedicated red indicator labelled “LOS” on the front or bottom panel.
  6. Give the device 2–3 minutes to fully initialise and attempt to acquire the optical signal. Optical signal acquisition can take longer than general startup.
  7. If the LOS light turns off and other indicators turn green or white, your connection has been restored. Test internet on a device.

If the LOS light remains red after 3 minutes, move to Fix 2.

2Inspect and Reseat the Green SC/APC Fibre Connector

This is the fix that resolves the LOS error for the majority of users who couldn’t fix it with a restart. The fibre optic connector — the small green-tipped plug where the thin fibre cable meets the ONT or router — is surprisingly easy to dislodge, and being even slightly unseated is enough to interrupt the light signal completely.

⚠️ Eye Safety — Read Before Proceeding: Never look directly into the end of a fibre optic cable or into the fibre port on your ONT or router — even when you believe the device is powered off. Active fibre optic cables carry invisible infrared laser light that can cause permanent retinal damage without any visible warning beam. Always keep fibre cable ends pointed away from your face and eyes at all times.
  1. Power off the JioFiber router or ONT by unplugging it from the wall socket. Wait 30 seconds.
  2. Locate the fibre optic cable where it enters the ONT or router. It connects via a small plug called an SC/APC connector — recognisable by its distinctive green-coloured tip and matching green port on the device.
  3. Look at the connection point carefully with your torch. Check whether the green connector is fully seated — it should be pushed all the way into the port with no visible gap between the connector body and the port entry.
  4. Grip the green connector body firmly (not the cable itself) and gently pull it straight out.
  5. Inspect the green tip of the connector. If you can see any dust, debris, or smudging on the polished end face, clean it by gently wiping it once with a dry lint-free cloth in a straight motion — never in circles.
  6. Don't use compressed air, water, cotton swabs, or any liquid on the fibre connector. Only a dry lint-free cloth should ever touch the connector end.
  7. Reinsert the green connector firmly and straight into its port until it clicks into place. Do not force it at an angle.
  8. Plug the power adapter back in and observe the LOS light. If it clears, the connection was the entire problem.
The SC/APC green connector is the most critical home-side component in any JioFiber LOS fix. The correct steps are pull straight, wipe once with a dry lint-free cloth if needed, then reinsert firmly and straight until it clicks. Any deviation — angled pull, liquid cleaning, cotton swabs — risks additional damage to the optical connector face.
3Trace the Fibre Cable for Damage or Tight Bends

If reseating the connector didn’t help, physically inspect the entire length of the fibre cable running from the ONT into your home. A kinked or sharply bent cable breaks the internal glass fibre — producing exactly the same LOS signal as a disconnected cable.

  1. Start at the ONT or router and follow the fibre cable with your eyes and torch along its entire visible path — typically running along walls, through cable clips, around door frames, and entering through a wall port.
  2. Look specifically for any point where the cable:
    • Makes a sharp bend or kink — fibre cables must never be bent tighter than approximately 30 mm radius (about the diameter of a ₹10 coin). Any tighter bend risks cracking the glass core.
    • Has been pinched under furniture, a door frame, or a cable clip that is too tight.
    • Shows any visible white or bright spot along the cable — a cracked fibre sometimes glows at the damage point because light leaks out.
    • Has been pulled or stretched — fibre cables are not designed to bear tension along their length.
  3. If you find a kinked or sharply bent section, gently straighten the cable — it may still be functional if the glass fibre hasn’t fully cracked.
  4. If the cable has a visible white spot, a crimp, or a bend that held shape when straightened, the fibre is physically damaged and must be replaced by a Jio technician. A damaged fibre cable cannot be spliced or repaired at home.
  5. After straightening any found bends, restart the ONT (Fix 1) to check whether the LOS clears.
The back panel of the JioFiber router where the fibre cable, LAN ports, USB ports, and reset button are located. When inspecting for Fix 3, trace the fibre cable from this back-panel connection point along its entire route to the wall entry — looking for any sharp bends, cable clips that are too tight, or pinch points under furniture or door frames.
4Check for a Jio Network Outage in Your Area

Before concluding the problem is with your equipment, spend two minutes confirming whether Jio is experiencing a service disruption in your area. A node-level outage at Jio’s OLT equipment causes every customer connected to that node to show LOS simultaneously — and no amount of cable inspection or restarting will restore the connection until Jio’s network is repaired.

  1. On your mobile phone using Jio mobile data (not Wi-Fi — your broadband is down), open the MyJio app.
  2. Navigate to Help > JioFiber > Report Issue — the app will check your account status and often automatically detect whether a network outage has been flagged in your area.
  3. Alternatively, call Jio customer care at 1800-889-9999 (toll-free, 24 hours) and ask the agent to check whether there is an outage or maintenance scheduled on your specific OLT node.
  4. Check social media — search “JioFiber down” or “JioFiber LOS” on Twitter/X to see whether other users in your city or locality are reporting the same issue simultaneously.
  5. If an outage is confirmed: Note the expected restoration time. The LOS light will clear automatically when Jio restores the signal — nothing further is needed on your end.
  6. If no outage is reported and neighbours with JioFiber have working internet: the issue is specific to your line or equipment. Continue to Fix 5.
ⓘ Save these numbers: Jio customer care 1800-889-9999 (toll-free, 24×7) and 198 from your Jio number. When calling, specify “JioFiber LOS red light blinking” — this routes your complaint to the broadband technical team rather than the general Jio mobile support queue.
5Inspect the External Wall Fibre Entry Connection Point

JioFiber installations include a connection point on the external wall of your home — where the fibre cable that runs from the street enters your premises. This junction, sometimes called the FAT (Fibre Access Terminal) connection or the SC connector at the wall entry point, can work loose after heavy rains, wind, or during wall cleaning.

⚠️ Safety First: Don't attempt to climb ladders, access rooftop cable runs, or work at height to inspect external cable connections. The external portions of the JioFiber installation are Jio’s infrastructure and are the sole responsibility of Jio’s field technicians. Only inspect the external wall connection point if it is safely accessible at ground level without a ladder. Never pull or cut any fibre cable exiting your premises.
  1. Locate the point on your home’s external wall where the fibre cable enters — usually a small wall-mounted enclosure or a simple pass-through hole with a connector visible near it.
  2. If this connection point is at a safe, accessible height (reachable without a ladder), inspect whether the fibre connector at this junction is fully seated. It will have the same green SC/APC connector as the one at the ONT inside.
  3. If you can safely reseat the external connector, do so using the same technique as Fix 2 — pull straight out, wipe once with a lint-free cloth if accessible, reinsert straight until it clicks.
  4. Return inside and restart the ONT (Fix 1) to check whether the LOS light clears.
  5. If the external connection point is not safely accessible at ground level, do not attempt to reach it — book a Jio technician visit directly.
This is what your JioFiber ONT should look like after a successful fix — a solid green indicator light at the bottom of the device, indicating the optical signal from Jio’s network is being received correctly and internet connectivity is restored. If you achieve this green light after any of the fixes above, test internet immediately on a connected device.

When to Call Jio

The JioFiber LOS error is one of those problems that is either completely fixable at home in a few minutes, or completely in Jio’s hands to fix. Stop troubleshooting and raise a service request when:

  • The fibre cable is visibly damaged — kinked, cut, crushed, or showing a bright glow point. Fibre cable cannot be repaired at home. A Jio technician will need to run a new cable segment, which is covered under the JioFiber installation warranty.
  • All home-side fixes have been completed (connector reseated, cable inspected, device restarted) and the LOS light is still blinking with no reported outage. The fault is likely at Jio’s splitter or OLT — only their field team can access and repair it.
  • The LOS light clears temporarily but returns within a few hours or days. Intermittent LOS is a classic symptom of a degraded fibre splice or a connector accumulating contamination at a junction point outside your home. Only a field technician with optical power measurement equipment can locate and fix an intermittent signal loss precisely.
  • The ONT device itself shows signs of hardware failure — such as no lights at all, visible physical damage, or error codes other than LOS blinking. The ONT is Jio’s equipment provided on loan as part of the JioFiber subscription — faulty hardware is Jio’s responsibility to replace at no charge.

How to raise a JioFiber complaint:

  • MyJio App: Open the app > Help > JioFiber > Raise a Complaint. Field technician visits are typically scheduled within 24–48 hours for LOS complaints in urban areas.
  • Call: 1800-889-9999 (toll-free, 24 hours, 7 days). Specify “JioFiber LOS red light blinking” clearly.
  • Jio Store: Visit your nearest Jio store in person if the complaint is not resolved within 48 hours of logging.

When speaking to Jio support, have your JioFiber account number (on the MyJio app under “My Account”), your registered mobile number, and confirm that the LOS light is specifically the one blinking red.

Frequently Asked Questions

LOS stands for Loss of Signal — specifically the optical signal that travels through the fibre optic cable from Jio’s network to your home. Your JioFiber ONT or router operates by detecting pulses of laser light in the fibre cable; when those pulses stop arriving, the LOS indicator turns on and blinks red. The blinking (rather than a solid red) indicates the device is powered and functional but is actively trying to detect the missing signal. This is different from a hardware fault, which would typically show different error codes or no lights at all. The most common causes of LOS are a loose SC/APC connector, a physically damaged fibre cable, or an outage on Jio’s network node.

Most JioFiber LOS errors can be resolved at home without a technician. The two most effective home fixes are a proper 60-second power restart (Fix 1) and reseating the green SC/APC fibre connector at the back of the ONT or router (Fix 2). Together, these two fixes resolve the LOS error for the large majority of users. You will need a Jio technician when the fibre cable itself is physically damaged (visible kink, white glow spot, or a crease that holds shape), when the LOS persists after all home-side checks with no reported outage, or when the fault is at Jio’s external infrastructure. A Jio technician visit is free when the fault is on Jio’s side or if the ONT hardware itself has failed.

The green connector on your JioFiber cable is called an SC/APC connector — SC (Subscriber Connector) describes the push-and-click plug mechanism, and APC (Angled Physical Contact) refers to the 8-degree angled polish on the end face of the connector tip. The green colour is the international standard colour-coding for APC-type fibre connectors, which is why all JioFiber home installation connectors are green. This connector is critical because it is the physical point where the optical light signal transfers from the fibre cable into the ONT’s optical receiver. Even a 1–2mm gap in the seating, or any dust on the polished end face, can reduce the received signal to zero — triggering LOS — even though the cable appears connected. Always inspect this connector first after a basic restart fails.

An LOS error that clears and then returns intermittently (every few hours or days) is one of the more frustrating JioFiber problems because it can seem like the restart “fixed” it when it didn’t. The three most common causes of intermittent LOS are: (1) a connector that is not fully seated — it reconnects after being disturbed by the restart process but not reliably; try pushing the green SC/APC connector more firmly into its port; (2) a partially damaged fibre — a microcrack in the glass core that allows signal through sometimes but not consistently; only a technician with optical power measurement equipment can confirm this; (3) temperature-related expansion/contraction of a connector or splice joint at an external junction point. Intermittent LOS that persists after confirming the home-side connector is fully seated should always be logged as a Jio complaint — it requires a field technician with the right test equipment to diagnose and fix reliably.

For LOS complaints raised through the MyJio app or by calling 1800-889-9999, Jio’s service level agreement (SLA) for JioFiber broadband faults is typically 24–48 hours in metro and Tier-1 cities, and 48–72 hours in smaller cities and towns. If the issue is an area-level outage (affecting multiple customers on the same OLT node), Jio usually resolves these within 4–8 hours as they are treated as network-priority incidents. When raising a complaint, ask the agent to note it as “fibre LOS, no internet, red light blinking” and request the complaint reference number so you can follow up if the deadline passes. If the complaint is not resolved within the promised timeline, visit your nearest Jio Store in person — escalations done in person are typically actioned faster than phone follow-ups.

A JioFiber LOS error affects all services delivered over the JioFiber connection simultaneously — broadband internet, JioTV+ (if using a JioFiber Set Top Box), and any VoIP phone service on the JioFiber plan. This is because all these services travel over the same optical fibre cable. When the LOS error occurs, the ONT loses the fibre signal entirely, meaning no data of any kind can be received or transmitted. Your Wi-Fi network will still be visible on your phone and laptop (the router is still powered and broadcasting Wi-Fi), but no device will have actual internet access. JioTV+ content that was cached for offline viewing may still be accessible, but any live TV, OTT streaming, or internet browsing will not work until the LOS error is resolved.
