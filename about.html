Welcome to Fix The Logic

If you are here, chances are something in your home or office is beeping, flashing a red light, or displaying a confusing error code — and you just want it to work again.

Modern appliances and gadgets are smarter than ever, but when they break, they are incredibly frustrating to fix. Official user manuals are often lost, outdated, or written in confusing technical jargon that simply tells you to “contact support.”

That is why Fix The Logic was created in 2026.

Our Mission

Our goal is simple: to translate confusing error codes into clear, step-by-step, DIY troubleshooting guides that anyone can follow. We believe that you should not have to pay a ₹500 service fee just to find out your washing machine’s water inlet filter needed a five-minute cleaning.

Fix The Logic is an independent troubleshooting database, not affiliated with any specific manufacturer. We write what actually works — based on real research, verified fixes, and genuine user experience. No sponsored “solutions,” no upsells, no filler.

What We Cover

We provide extensively researched, human-written solutions across four main areas:

🏠

Home Appliances

Washing machines, refrigerators, dishwashers, and AC units. Error codes from IFB, Samsung, LG, Bosch, Whirlpool, Haier, and more.

🖨

Office Tech

Stubborn printer errors, offline queues, driver failures, and network setup issues across HP, Canon, Epson, and Brother.

📶

Smart Home Devices

Routers, smart speakers, displays, and connected devices. Amazon Echo, Google Nest, Ring, and Wi-Fi connectivity fixes.

💻

Gadgets & Software

Wearables, earbuds, browser errors, connection timeouts, and system glitches. Windows, macOS, and everyday app problems.

Our Commitment to Safety and Trust

Every guide on our site includes necessary safety warnings. We will always tell you when to unplug a device — and more importantly, we will tell you when a problem is too dangerous for a DIY fix and requires a certified professional.

We never recommend a repair that carries a safety risk without making that risk explicit. Your safety comes before a completed fix, every single time.
