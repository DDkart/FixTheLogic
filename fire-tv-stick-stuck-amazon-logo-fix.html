Fire TV Stick Stuck on Amazon Logo: The Most Common Causes and Fixes

A Fire TV Stick looping on the Amazon logo is a power problem more often than a software problem. The stick requires at least 1A (5V) to boot fully, and the USB port on most TVs supplies only 500mA — just enough to show the logo but not enough to complete the boot sequence. Using the included power adapter in a wall outlet, not the TV's USB port, resolves this immediately.

💬 From experiencePower supply is the first thing I check on any Fire Stick that won't boot. The USB port on your TV almost certainly isn't delivering enough current. The included Amazon adapter plugged into a wall outlet is the correct setup — not because Amazon is being fussy, but because the hardware genuinely needs that current to boot cleanly.

What Does “Stuck on the Amazon Logo” Mean?

Said plainly, your Fire TV Stick is frozen during the boot sequence — the startup process it runs every time it powers on — and cannot get past the initial loading stage.

When you plug in a Fire TV Stick, it goes through a specific sequence of startup steps: the bootloader initialises the hardware, Fire OS loads from internal storage, system services start up one by one, and finally the home screen interface launches. The Amazon logo is displayed during the early and middle stages of that sequence. When it freezes there and never progresses, something has blocked one of those middle steps.

The four most common reasons this happens:

  • A Fire OS software update was interrupted — by a power cut, accidental unplug, or network dropout — leaving the operating system in a partially updated state that can neither finish the update nor roll back to a working version. This is the single most common cause.
  • The Fire Stick is powered from a TV’s USB port instead of the Amazon wall adapter. TV USB ports deliver only 500 mA — half the current the Fire Stick needs during the most power-intensive boot operations. Enough to show the logo; not enough to complete startup.
  • Internal storage corruption — from an abrupt shutdown or a prolonged power outage — preventing Fire OS from loading the files it needs.
  • A corrupted app or system cache blocking the startup routine — particularly common after a long period without restarting or after a problematic app update.

In the the majority of of cases, a proper power reset or a factory reset will have the device working again in minutes.

This is what your Fire TV Stick home screen should look like after a successful boot — if yours is frozen on the Amazon logo before reaching this screen, the boot sequence has stalled. One of the five fixes in this guide will get you here.

What You’ll Need

You need almost nothing for this fix — everything is already in your living room:

  • Your Fire TV Stick and the Amazon-supplied power adapter and USB cable (important — not the TV’s USB port)
  • The Fire TV remote with working batteries
  • A TV with an HDMI port
  • About 5–15 minutes of your time

No screwdrivers, no computers, no downloads required for Fixes 1–4.

5 Fixes — Ordered by Success Rate

Try each fix in sequence, starting from the top. Fix 1 or Fix 2 resolves this for most people.

1Perform a Proper Power Reset — 60 Seconds, Not 5

The critical difference between a standard unplug-and-replug and a proper power reset is how long you wait. Simply unplugging and replugging often lands you back at the same frozen logo because a partially loaded state gets restored from cache. A full 60-second discharge forces a complete cold start.

  1. Unplug the Fire TV Stick’s power adapter from the wall socket completely — not from the HDMI port, and not just from the USB port on the stick itself. Pull the plug from the wall.
  2. Wait a full 60 seconds. This is longer than the standard advice but ensures the Fire Stick’s capacitors fully discharge and any held state in RAM is cleared completely.
  3. While waiting, remove the batteries from the remote and reinsert them — a remote with weak batteries can occasionally interfere with the startup handshake.
  4. Plug the power adapter back into the wall socket and reconnect to the Fire Stick.
  5. Watch the Amazon logo carefully — if the boot sequence progresses past the logo to a loading bar or directly to the home screen, the reset worked.
  6. If the logo freezes again within 60–90 seconds of startup, move to Fix 2.
2Switch to the Amazon Wall Adapter — Not Your TV’s USB Port

This fix resolves the frozen logo screen for a surprisingly large number of users — particularly those who have the Fire Stick plugged directly into one of the TV’s USB ports for convenience.

Fire TV Sticks require a stable 5V / 1A (1000 mA) power supply to boot successfully. Most TV USB ports deliver only 500 mA — exactly half the current the Fire Stick needs during its most power-intensive boot operations. This is just enough power to start the boot sequence and display the logo, but not enough to power the Wi-Fi chip, storage controller, and processor simultaneously during the later boot stages. The result looks exactly like a software freeze but is actually a power starvation issue.

  1. If the Fire Stick is currently plugged into your TV’s USB port, unplug it entirely.
  2. Connect the Fire Stick to the Amazon-supplied power adapter (the small white plug from the box) and plug that adapter directly into a wall socket.
  3. Keep the Fire Stick connected to the TV’s HDMI port as normal.
  4. Power on and observe whether the boot sequence now completes fully.
⚠️ No original adapter? Any USB power adapter rated at 5V / 1A or higher will work — a modern phone charger with these specifications is a perfect substitute. Don't use older 500 mA adapters, USB hubs, or power banks with automatic sleep modes.
The frozen Amazon logo caused by insufficient power is visually identical to a software crash — which is why so many users spend hours troubleshooting software when the solution is simply switching from the TV’s USB port to a proper 5V / 1A wall adapter.
3Force Restart Using the Remote Button Combination

If the power reset didn’t help and the correct adapter is already in use, Fire TV Sticks support a hardware-level forced restart using only the remote — a button combination that sends a direct restart signal to the device, bypassing the normal software shutdown sequence.

  1. Make sure the Fire TV Stick is plugged in and displaying the stuck Amazon logo.
  2. Point the remote directly at the Fire TV Stick (not at the TV — the Alexa Voice Remote’s hardware signals go toward the stick).
  3. Press and hold the Select button (the circular button in the centre of the navigation ring) and the Play/Pause button simultaneously.
  4. Hold both buttons for 10 seconds. Do not release early.
  5. The Fire Stick will force-reboot — the screen will go black briefly before the Amazon logo reappears.
  6. Watch whether the boot sequence progresses past the logo this time.
For Fix 3: hold Select + Play/Pause for 10 seconds to force-restart. For Fix 4: hold Back + Right arrow for 10 seconds to trigger factory reset — both combinations work even while the device is frozen on the Amazon logo screen.
4Factory Reset Using the Remote Button Combination

If three proper restarts haven’t broken the loop, the underlying issue is almost certainly corrupted system files or a failed OS update that has left Fire OS in an unrecoverable state. A factory reset wipes the device completely and reinstalls a clean version of Fire OS from scratch.

⚠️ What a factory reset erases: All downloaded apps, login credentials, personalised settings, watchlists, and downloaded content for offline viewing. What it does NOT erase: Your Amazon account, purchase history, Prime subscriptions, and app purchase records — all safely stored in Amazon’s cloud and restored automatically when you sign back in. Apps will need to be reinstalled; cloud content is immediately available.

Method A — While stuck on the logo (recommended):

  1. With the Fire Stick displaying the stuck Amazon logo, point the remote directly at the device.
  2. Press and hold the Back button (curved arrow) and the Right directional button (on the navigation ring) simultaneously.
  3. Hold both buttons for 10 seconds.
  4. A factory reset confirmation screen should appear — select “Continue” using the Select button to confirm the reset.
  5. The device will restart and display a fresh setup screen identical to when it was new.

Method B — Via Settings menu (if device partially boots):

  1. Navigate to Settings > My Fire TV > Reset to Factory Defaults.
  2. Confirm when prompted.

After the factory reset completes:

  1. Connect to your Wi-Fi network, sign in with your Amazon account, and allow the device to complete initial setup and download any pending Fire OS updates.
  2. Keep it plugged into the wall adapter throughout setup — do not use the TV’s USB port during this process.
When the remote button combination successfully triggers a restart or factory reset, you’ll see a brief “Your Fire TV is turning off” screen before it reboots — this confirms the reset command was received. If the screen never transitions from the frozen logo, the remote may need new batteries or may need re-pairing.
Keep this reference card handy: all four button combinations work while the Fire TV Stick is frozen on the logo. Always point the remote directly at the stick itself, not the TV, and hold for the full 10 seconds without releasing early.
5Re-flash Fire OS Using ADB — Advanced Last Resort

If the factory reset via remote is not triggering — the device appears completely unresponsive to all button combinations — Fire OS itself may be so corrupted that it cannot respond to remote inputs. In this situation, it is possible to re-flash the Fire OS firmware using a Windows computer and Amazon’s ADB (Android Debug Bridge) tools.

⚠️ Advanced users only: This process involves ADB command-line tools and manually flashing firmware files. If performed incorrectly it can leave the Fire Stick in a worse state. If you are not comfortable with basic command-line instructions, skip directly to “When to Contact Amazon” — Amazon’s replacement process under warranty is fast and free.
  1. Download the Android Debug Bridge (ADB) tools from the official Android developer site.
  2. Download the correct Fire OS firmware file for your specific Fire Stick model from Amazon’s developer portal.
  3. Connect the Fire Stick to your Windows computer via a USB OTG cable while it is also connected to power.
  4. Use ADB command-line commands to push the firmware file to the device and initiate the flash.
  5. For the most current step-by-step instructions specific to your Fire Stick generation, search “Fire TV Stick ADB re-flash [your model]” on AFTVnews.com — this site maintains the most accurate and up-to-date firmware flashing guides for every Fire Stick model.

When to Contact Amazon

Fire TV Sticks are sealed consumer devices with no user-serviceable internal parts. When the device itself has a hardware fault, the correct path is Amazon’s support and warranty replacement process:

  • No signs of life at all — no logo, no light, nothing on screen even with the correct power adapter and a confirmed working HDMI port. Test the HDMI port with another device first.
  • The factory reset completes but the device freezes on the logo again immediately on the very next boot, before any apps are installed. This indicates a hardware fault — most likely a failed internal storage chip — that no software fix can address.
  • The device is physically damaged — cracked, exposed to moisture, or showing burn marks near the USB connector.
  • Your Fire TV Stick is within its warranty period. Amazon offers a 1-year limited warranty on Fire TV Stick devices. A device that cannot be recovered with a factory reset is eligible for a free replacement — typically shipped within 2–3 business days.

To reach Amazon device support:

  • US: amazon.com/devicesupport
  • India: amazon.in/devicesupport
  • Or log into your Amazon account → Returns & Orders > Get help with a device

Amazon’s device support team can remotely diagnose Fire TV Sticks connected to your account. Have your device’s serial number ready — it’s printed on the back of the Fire Stick and also visible in your Amazon account under Manage Your Content and Devices.

Frequently Asked Questions

A factory reset erases locally stored data only — downloaded apps, app login states, saved settings, and any content downloaded for offline viewing. It does not erase your Amazon account, your purchase history, your Prime Video subscriptions, or app purchase records — all of these are stored in Amazon’s cloud. When you sign back into your Amazon account after the reset, your purchased apps appear in the “Your Apps & Games” section ready to reinstall, and all your cloud content is immediately accessible. The setup takes about 5–10 minutes to restore to a usable state.

The most common reason remote button combinations don’t work on a stuck Fire Stick is low or dead batteries — replace them first before attempting any button combination. The second most common cause is the remote having lost its Bluetooth pairing with the stick — you can re-pair by holding the Home button for 10 seconds. Also ensure you are pointing the remote directly at the Fire Stick (not the TV), within 3 metres, with no obstructions. If the remote has completely lost pairing, try holding Home for 10 seconds and then immediately try the factory reset combination (Back + Right) again.

An interrupted Fire OS update is the most common cause of the logo freeze, and unfortunately a proper power reset followed by a factory reset is usually the only reliable fix when the update corruption is complete. The power reset (Fix 1) occasionally allows a partially completed update to finish if the interruption was very brief — try it first with a full 60-second discharge. If that doesn’t work, the factory reset (Fix 4) will wipe the corrupted update and re-install a clean OS version. To prevent this recurring, ensure the Fire Stick is always plugged into the wall adapter (never the TV USB port) so it never loses power during an update, and avoid unplugging or power-cycling the device when the orange update light is visible on the stick itself.

On a healthy Fire TV Stick, the Amazon logo with the loading animation typically displays for 45 seconds to 2 minutes before the home screen appears — this varies depending on the model generation (older 1st/2nd gen sticks are slower) and how many apps are installed. The logo will usually disappear and the home screen content will start loading at 60–90 seconds on a 3rd gen or later device. If the logo is still showing after 3 minutes with no progress, the boot has stalled and you should begin the fixes in this guide.

Switching HDMI ports won’t fix a frozen logo screen — the HDMI port only carries the video output, which is working fine (that’s why you can see the logo). The boot freeze is happening inside the Fire Stick’s processor and storage, not in the HDMI connection. However, it is worth briefly trying a different HDMI port only if the Fire Stick appears completely dead with no image at all — in that specific case, a faulty HDMI port or HDMI-CEC conflict on the original port could be preventing the display from showing any image. For a visible but frozen logo, HDMI port changes have no effect.

A logo freeze that returns repeatedly after a factory reset points to one of three underlying causes: (1) The device is still powered from a TV USB port — switch to the wall adapter permanently; (2) the internal storage (eMMC) chip is degrading — this is a hardware fault that worsens over time, and the factory reset works temporarily until storage corruption accumulates again; or (3) a specific app is reinstalling and corrupting the boot sequence — after the reset, reinstall apps one at a time and observe which reinstall triggers the next freeze. If the freeze returns even without any apps installed, the device has a hardware fault and Amazon’s warranty replacement process is the appropriate next step.
