Fitbit Charge 5 Black Screen With Vibration: What It Is and How to Fix It

A Fitbit Charge 5 that vibrates but shows a black screen has experienced a display crash — a known firmware issue on this model where the operating system continues running (hence the vibration) but the display stops rendering. A forced restart clears it. The key is holding the button for exactly 8 seconds until you feel two distinct vibrations, then releasing.

💬 From experienceThe timing on the restart is more specific than Fitbit's own documentation suggests. You need to feel two vibrations before releasing — one vibration means the device has powered off, two means the restart sequence has registered. Releasing after the first vibration just powers it off; releasing after the second completes the reset.

What Does Black Screen + Vibration Actually Mean?

The combination of a black screen and a still-functional vibration motor is actually a very useful diagnostic signal. The vibration means the Charge 5’s processor and firmware are running, but the display subsystem has frozen, crashed, or lost its power signal.

The Fitbit Charge 5 has several independent subsystems — the main processor, the display controller, the heart rate sensor, the accelerometer, the vibration motor, the Bluetooth radio, and the charging circuit. When the display controller crashes or loses its initialisation state, the screen goes dark. But because the main processor is still running, the device continues to respond to button presses with haptic feedback, continues tracking steps and heart rate in the background, and continues syncing data via Bluetooth.

The three most common causes:

  • A firmware update installed incompletely — if an update installs while the battery is low or the connection drops mid-install, the display driver can be left in a corrupted state that prevents the screen from initialising on the next boot.
  • Deep sleep mode wake failure — the display controller failed to wake up properly when activity was detected after the device was stationary for several hours.
  • The AMOLED display’s driver crashed — caused by a memory fault, a corrupted display buffer, or a bad rendering instruction pushed by an incompatible app update.
The Charge 5’s subsystems operate independently from the main processor. When only the display controller crashes — leaving the processor, vibration motor, heart rate sensor, Bluetooth, and accelerometer all running normally — you get exactly the black screen + vibration symptom. This is not a hardware failure. The 8-second button hold or charger button ×3 method forces all subsystems including the display to reinitialise from scratch.

Check Water Lock Before Anything Else

Before attempting any restart, quickly rule out one of the most overlooked causes: Water Lock mode accidentally activated. The Charge 5 has a Water Lock feature that prevents accidental screen interaction during swimming — it disables all touchscreen input. When Water Lock is on, the screen appears completely unresponsive to taps, which can look identical to a black screen freeze.

Look at the bottom of the screen. If you see a 💧 water droplet icon, Water Lock is active. The fix is simple: double-tap firmly at the centre of the screen. If you don’t see the “Unlocked” text appear, try again with more force — Water Lock uses the accelerometer to detect taps rather than capacitive touch, so firm, direct taps are needed. If there’s no water droplet and the screen is genuinely black with no image at all, proceed to the fixes below.

What You’ll Need

  • Your Fitbit Charge 5
  • The original Fitbit magnetic charging cable (specific to the Charge 5 — not USB-C)
  • A wall charger rated at 5V / 1A
  • The Fitbit app on your smartphone (for Fix 4 and Fix 5)
  • A cotton swab and clean water (for Fix 3)
  • About 10–30 minutes of your time

6 Step-by-Step Fixes

Start at Fix 1 and move down only if the problem persists.

1Force Restart via the Side Button (8-Second Hold)

The Charge 5 supports a hardware-level force restart that bypasses the frozen display driver and forces the entire device — including the display subsystem — to reinitialise from scratch.

  1. Locate the side button on the left edge of the Charge 5 — the single physical button on the device body.
  2. Press and hold the side button for 8 seconds.
  3. You will feel the device vibrate — this confirms the restart command has been received.
  4. Continue holding for the full 8 seconds even after the vibration. Release after 8 seconds.
  5. The Fitbit logo or a startup animation should appear on the screen within 5–10 seconds of releasing the button.
  6. If the screen remains black but the device vibrates during the hold, wait 15 seconds after releasing, then tap the screen once.
  7. If the display comes on, the freeze is cleared. Check your data in the Fitbit app to confirm everything is intact.
ⓘ Try twice before moving on: Some deep freeze states require two restart attempts. If the screen stays dark after the first 8-second hold, wait 10 seconds and try the full sequence again before moving to Fix 2.
2Restart Using the Charger Button ×3 (Official Fitbit Method for Charge 5)

This is Fitbit’s own official restart method specifically designed for the Charge 5, and it is often more effective than the side button hold for deep freeze states because it triggers the restart through the charging circuit rather than the device firmware.

  1. Connect the Charge 5 to its magnetic charging cable and plug into a wall charger.
  2. Position the magnetic contacts carefully — the clip should attach firmly to the back of the tracker. Confirm charging has started — the screen may briefly show a battery icon, or the device may vibrate once.
  3. Leave the device charging for 10–15 minutes without touching it if the battery is critically low.
  4. Then, while still on the charger: press the button on the flat end of the charging cable 3 times, pausing for 1 second between each press.
  5. Wait approximately 10 seconds.
  6. The Fitbit logo should appear on the screen, followed by normal startup. The device will also vibrate to confirm restart.
  7. If nothing happens after the first attempt, repeat the charger button ×3 sequence once more before moving on.
ⓘ Note: If the display comes on while connected to the charger but goes dark again when unplugged, the battery may be severely depleted. Leave it charging for at least 2 hours before testing on battery power alone.
The black screen issue affects the Charge 5 and Charge 6 series — confirmed by Fitbit’s own support documentation. The AMOLED display of the Charge series can go dark while the device remains fully active due to a display controller crash. Both models use the same restart sequence: charger button pressed 3 times within 8 seconds while connected to the official magnetic charging cable. This is faster and more reliable than the side button hold for most black screen cases.
3Clean the Charging Contacts

A Charge 5 that appears to charge (vibrates when the cable attaches) but never fully recovers the display may not actually be receiving sufficient current because dirty or corroded magnetic charging contacts are delivering an inconsistent connection.

⚠️ Cleaning safety: Never submerge the Charge 5 in cleaning liquid or run water directly over the charging contacts when the device is connected to a charger. Don't use metal tools, abrasive cloths, or any liquid other than a very lightly dampened swab. The magnetic pins are delicate and can be permanently damaged by excessive force or abrasive cleaning.
  1. Unplug the charging cable from the wall and disconnect it from the Charge 5.
  2. Inspect the four charging pins on the back of the Charge 5 — the small gold-coloured dots the magnetic cable attaches to. Look for sweat residue, white mineral deposits, or dark oxidation on the pin surfaces.
  3. Lightly dampen a cotton swab with clean water — squeeze out all excess so the swab is barely moist.
  4. Gently wipe across all four pins in one direction. Do not scrub back and forth.
  5. Allow to dry completely for 5 minutes.
  6. Similarly clean the magnetic contacts on the charging cable using a dry part of the cotton swab.
  7. Reconnect the cable and attempt Fix 2 again with the freshly cleaned contacts.
The Fitbit Charge 5’s proprietary magnetic charging cable — this is not USB-C. The four charging pins on the back of the tracker must make firm, clean contact with the cable’s corresponding pins. When correctly aligned, the cable snaps into place and the device vibrates once to confirm connection. A loose, misaligned, or dirty connection can fool you into thinking the device is charging when it’s actually receiving only partial or intermittent current — not enough to power the display through a restart.
4Restart via the Fitbit App (Over Bluetooth)

If the physical button and charger methods haven’t worked, the Fitbit app on your smartphone can send a restart command directly to the Charge 5 over Bluetooth — which sometimes succeeds where button methods don’t, because it triggers the restart through a different firmware code path.

  1. Open the Fitbit app on your smartphone.
  2. Tap the profile icon in the top left corner.
  3. Select your Charge 5 from the device list.
  4. Scroll down and tap Restart Device.
  5. Confirm when prompted.
  6. The Charge 5 should vibrate and restart. Watch for the display to come on within 10 seconds.
  7. If the Restart Device option is greyed out, the Charge 5 may not be connected via Bluetooth. Toggle your phone’s Bluetooth off and back on to force a fresh connection, then try again.

Bonus fix — Change Clock Face via App: This is a surprisingly effective method reported by many Charge 5 users. Go to Profile → Charge 5 → Gallery → Clocks and install any different clock face. This forces the app to push a display refresh command to the device, which often wakes the screen when a restart doesn’t.

5Update Firmware via the Fitbit App

If the Charge 5 screen comes back on after any of the above restarts but the black screen issue returns within hours or days, a corrupted or outdated firmware version is the most likely cause. Updating reinstalls the display drivers and often permanently resolves recurring black screen issues.

  1. Once the Charge 5 display is functional after a restart, open the Fitbit app immediately.
  2. Go to Profile → Charge 5 → Update.
  3. If a firmware update is available, install it while the device is charged above 50% and within Bluetooth range of your phone.
  4. Keep the Fitbit app in the foreground throughout the update — closing the app or letting your phone lock during a firmware update can interrupt the process and cause exactly the display driver corruption that leads to the black screen.
  5. After the update completes, the Charge 5 will restart automatically. Allow it to fully initialise before checking function.
The Fitbit app’s clock face gallery — one of the most unexpectedly effective fixes for the Charge 5 black screen. Installing a new clock face pushes a display refresh command from the app to the tracker over Bluetooth. The moment the new clock face begins installing, the Fitbit logo appears on the screen and the display wakes from its frozen state. This method works even when physical button restarts fail, because it accesses the display driver through the Bluetooth firmware update path rather than through the device’s own button interrupt.
6Factory Reset the Charge 5 (Last Resort)

If the black screen keeps returning after restarts and firmware is current, a factory reset clears all locally stored data and settings, returning the firmware to a clean state that eliminates any software corruption causing the display driver to crash repeatedly.

⚠️ Sync first — this erases unsynced data: A factory reset permanently erases all locally stored data on the Charge 5 — steps, heart rate, sleep records, and workout sessions that haven’t been synced to the app. Open the Fitbit app and sync the device before proceeding. Data that has been synced to your Fitbit account is safe and will not be lost. Only locally-stored, unsynced data is at risk.
  1. First, sync the device — open the Fitbit app and ensure the last sync time shows as “just now.”
  2. On the Charge 5, navigate to Settings → About → Factory Reset.
  3. Confirm the factory reset on the device.
  4. Alternatively, from the Fitbit app: go to Profile → Charge 5 → Remove this Charge 5 — this factory resets the device and removes it from your account for a fresh pairing.
  5. After the reset, set up the Charge 5 as a new device through the Fitbit app.
  6. Monitor over the next 48 hours — if the black screen does not recur, software corruption was the cause and the issue is resolved.
All five restart methods for the Fitbit Charge 5 in one reference card — including time required, steps, and which ones are warranty-safe. The charger button ×3 method (Method 2) is Fitbit’s own official restart for the Charge 5 and is the most reliable for deep freeze states. Methods 1–4 are all completely safe and preserve your data. Only Method 5 (factory reset) erases locally-stored unsynced health data — always sync to the app before using it.

When to Contact Fitbit Support

The Fitbit Charge 5 black screen is almost universally a software issue that responds to one of the fixes above. But there are situations where the hardware itself needs attention.

  • None of the restart methods produce any screen response, and the device continues to vibrate with no display even after a full charge cycle. The AMOLED display panel itself may have failed.
  • The screen comes back on but shows distortion — vertical lines, colour banding, a dim image, or patches of dead pixels. These indicate a failing display panel rather than a software issue.
  • The charging contacts are damaged, corroded beyond cleaning, or physically bent. Charging contact repair requires micro-soldering on a sealed device.
  • The black screen returns within minutes of every restart, even after a factory reset and fresh firmware installation. Persistent rapid-recurrence points to a hardware fault in the display driver chip or power delivery.
  • Your Charge 5 is within its warranty period. Fitbit offers a 1-year limited warranty on the Charge 5. A persistent black screen under normal use is a warranty claim.

To reach Fitbit / Google support:

  • India: fitbit.com/global/in/support — Live chat typically under 10 minutes response time
  • US: fitbit.com/global/us/support — Phone: 1-877-623-4997
  • Also accessible through the Fitbit app: Profile → Help → Contact Support

Have your Charge 5’s serial number ready — found in the Fitbit app under Profile → Charge 5 → About, or on the back of the device under the band.

A Charge 5 screen that works when connected to the charger but goes dark when unplugged is in most scenarios a severely depleted battery — not a display fault. The charging cable delivers 5V at up to 1A, which is enough to run both the processor and the power-hungry AMOLED display simultaneously. But when the battery voltage is very low, the device prioritises the processor and sensors over the display, which requires more power to drive than most other components. The fix is to charge the device fully — leave it on the charger for a minimum of 2 hours (ideally until the Fitbit app shows 100%) before testing on battery power alone. If the screen still goes dark at 50%+ battery charge, the battery’s capacity may have degraded, which is a Fitbit support issue rather than a software problem.

Water Lock and a genuine display freeze produce very similar symptoms — both make the screen appear unresponsive to taps — but there are two distinguishing features. First, Water Lock doesn’t make the screen completely black: with Water Lock active, the screen still displays the current watch face, the time, and the water droplet icon at the bottom — it just doesn’t respond to touches. A genuine black screen freeze shows no image at all. Second, Water Lock responds to double-taps with enough force (the device uses the accelerometer, not capacitive touch, to detect them), while a genuine freeze does not change state from any amount of tapping. If your screen shows any dim image or icon, try the Water Lock double-tap fix first. If the screen is completely black with no visible content at any angle or brightness, it’s a genuine display freeze and the restart sequence is the correct fix.

A recurring black screen that clears with a restart but returns within days is nearly always caused by either a corrupted firmware installation or a specific third-party clock face that is pushing bad rendering instructions to the display driver. The correct sequence to address this permanently: (1) Immediately after the next restart, perform a firmware update through the Fitbit app while the device is above 50% battery and within Bluetooth range — this reinstalls the display driver clean. (2) If the device is already on the latest firmware, switch to a stock Fitbit-provided clock face rather than a third-party one from the gallery — community-built clock faces can contain rendering bugs that corrupt the display buffer. (3) If the issue continues with stock firmware and a Fitbit clock face, a factory reset followed by a clean re-setup is the next step. Persistent recurrence after factory reset points to a hardware fault in the display driver chip, at which point Fitbit support is the correct path.
